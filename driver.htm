  • Again, the dash opening was not of the needed size. In my old car, we had
    repeated problems on hot days with the opening changing shape. Henry
    had a great idea to fix this in the red car. This got quite involved but the end
    result was better then expected. I never understood how he was going to
    do this even though he showed me more then once. He made a special
    cutting jig on the milling machine and lathe just for this car. (Why buy tools
    if you can make them) The end result is a factory look and very
    clean at that. Follow the pictures VERY CAREFULLY or you will miss a detail or so.
  • Photo Descriptions.
    Dash was removed and the opening size was drawn on a piece of plastic then
    transferred to a piece of aluminum.
    All corners were rounded and the final opening shape was done.
    Next, a steel ring was clamped tightly and welded as one.
    Henry then made a jig to fit the cutter that would cut the foam from under the
    dash while leaving the vinyl of the dash untouched.
    The fiberglass negative mold ring was used for tracing the pattern for the jig.
    Ring was then dropped into place for a nice snug fit and glued.
    Final picture is factory size and the new size.
    Bondo inserts were then made, sanded, then covered with factory BMW vinyl.
