• With two lifter motors and one turning motor, we needed something to
    control them. To do this with relays, it would have needed about 14 in total.
    Each speaker goes up at a certain time and comes down in a certain time
    and order. A safety circuit was built in just in case something went wrong
    or if the tweeter missed its mark. In the last, car we once had the pod almost
    go right through the windshield. This design eliminates that problem. Notice
    the double-sided design work and hand placed,  Surface Mounted Parts. All
      of this work was done under a magnifying glass with a steady hand in Henrys
    house. LED's were added to the board so we can follow every step of the
    board's functions for problems. I almost forgot that Henry did 100% of the
    design work on this board. Some of the computer layouts and diagrams are
    pictured below.
  • Photo Descriptions.
    First the board was designed on a CAD program. Another program then laid
    out the locations of all parts.
    Once this was finalized, Henry printed out a clear film of the board and then
    shot it under light like a picture.
    An impression was then left on the board.
    Next it was put into various chemicals to remove the unwanted copper and
    finally each hole was drilled out by hand for component placement.
