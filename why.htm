• Why do I use Zapco amplifiers?
    Let it be known that Robert Zeff is one of the great high end audiophile
    designers of the car audio industry. He is the head amplifier designer at Zapco.
    Open most amplifiers and you will see quality components. Open the Zapco
    and you will see all that plus surface
    mount components. This is expensive and very refined building. Just this
    one point separates Zapco from the rest. Some of you  are familiar with the
    Slew or rise time capability of an amplifier. This in car terms is 0-60.
    How many volts can an amplifier put out fast. The average slew rate for
    most amplifiers is about 20-30 volts per microsecond. The higher the number
    the better. You may notice that almost NONE of the major car amplifier
    companies publish this figure. Zapco's figure for the Z600 is 73 volts. Many
    high end home units have trouble matching this figure. This figures
    contributes to snappy sound. When it come to the sound, Robert uses the
  • Martin Logan  CLS II electrostatic speakers and Mark Levinson amplifiers
    as his reference. He is a real
    audiophile, designing real audiophile products for the car. About 3 years
    ago I hauled my old Zapco Z300 to a friend of mine's house that has a pair
    of Martin Logan Electrostatic speakers and Krell amplifiers. Toting a
    battery and the amp, we substituted the Zapco for the Krell. All of us had
    a tough time telling which was which. The Krell was $ 7200. The moral of
    the story, Zapco sounds good, built to advanced standards and their power
    ratings are very underrated. My last amp was rated at 150 watts per
    channel. We got it measured, and 254 watts per channel as the true figure.
    A great value.
